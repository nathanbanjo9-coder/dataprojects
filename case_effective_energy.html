Business Context

Why This Analysis Matters

Effective Energy Group delivers government-funded energy efficiency programmes across the UK, managing a large field workforce of installers, engineers, supervisors and logistics staff. Understanding the cost and wage structure is critical to maintaining programme profitability and operational efficiency.

Business Goal

OBJECTIVE · SCOPE

  • Understand labour cost structure across all UK regions
  • Identify overtime patterns and seasonal workforce pressure
  • Calculate true cost per installation by region and role
  • Benchmark productivity and efficiency across departments
  • Surface actionable recommendations to reduce operational costs

Key Questions

ANALYSIS FOCUS

  • Which regions have the highest cost per installation?
  • How much of total wages is driven by overtime?
  • Which roles generate the most output per pound spent?
  • Do travel costs significantly vary by region?
  • Are there seasonal patterns in overtime usage?

Key Findings

What the Data Revealed

Six data-driven insights extracted from the operational dataset — each directly relevant to Effective Energy Group's cost management and programme efficiency objectives.

01 Wales Highest Regional Cost

Wales carries the highest total operational cost at £451M, driven by a combination of wage pressure and above-average travel costs across a dispersed workforce.

02 East Anglia Most Efficient

East Anglia achieves the lowest cost per installation at £359.84 — 1.6% below the national average of £365.39 — indicating strong route density and workforce utilisation.

03 Operations Manager Wage Premium

Operations Managers average £31.60 per hour — 2.1x the rate of Insulation Installers — and carry the highest wage cost per installation at £3.6K, directly informing supervision cost strategy.

04 Insulation Drives Volume

Insulation Installers complete 1.88 million installations — the highest of any role — at a wage cost per install of just £119, making them the most cost-productive role in the workforce.

05 Winter Overtime Spikes

January and February show consistent overtime and installation peaks across all regions — driven by seasonal demand — creating predictable cost pressure that can be planned for in annual budgets.

06 Midlands Overtime Highest

Midlands has the highest overtime concentration at 10.51% of total wages — above the national average of 10.49% — suggesting scheduling inefficiency or chronic under-resourcing in peak periods.

Recommendations

Actionable Cost Reduction Opportunities

Five data-backed recommendations to reduce operational costs, improve workforce efficiency, and maintain installation programme profitability.

01 Winter Workforce Pre-Planning

Seasonal data shows consistent overtime spikes in October–February. Pre-hire temporary contractors in September to meet winter demand without relying on expensive overtime. Target a reduction of overtime from 10.49% to below 8% of total wages.

02 Regional Travel Route Optimisation

Wales and Scotland carry the highest travel cost as a proportion of total operational cost. A route density analysis and postcode-level scheduling review could reduce travel spend by 10–15%, with Scotland as the highest priority region.

03 Supervision Cost Review

Supervision carries the highest total wage cost at 20.74% of department spend, with Operations Managers averaging £31.60/hr. A span-of-control review — assessing how many installers each supervisor manages — could identify overstaffing at the management layer.

04 Replicate East Anglia Efficiency Model

East Anglia achieves the lowest cost per installation nationally. A root cause analysis of their scheduling, workforce mix and travel routing should be conducted and used as a benchmark model to improve higher-cost regions like Wales and Scotland.

05 Monthly Cost Per Install KPI Monitoring

Implement a monthly operational dashboard tracking cost per installation by region and role. Early warning thresholds should trigger management review when cost per install exceeds £370 — approximately 1.3% above the national average — to catch efficiency drift before it becomes material.

Limitations & Analytical Constraints

What This Analysis Does Not Cover

Acknowledging the boundaries of the analysis is as important as the findings themselves. The following constraints should be considered when acting on these insights.

Synthetic Dataset

DATA SOURCE · SIMULATION
Dataset is simulated — real operational data may show different patterns. Regional cost differences are modelled, not measured from actual operations. Validation against live payroll and job management systems would be required. A real analysis would begin with data quality checks and anomaly detection.

No Margin or Revenue Data

DATA GAP · PROFITABILITY
Analysis covers costs only — not revenue or programme funding received. Cannot determine whether high-cost regions are still profitable. True profitability analysis would require ECO funding value per installation. Cost per install alone does not indicate whether a programme is commercially viable.

No Individual Performance Data

GRANULARITY · WORKFORCE
Analysis is aggregated by role and region — not by individual contractor. Cannot identify specific underperforming employees or contractors. A real system would include contractor ID-level performance tracking. Absence data, sickness, and attrition not modelled in this dataset.

No External Cost Factors

CONTEXT · ENVIRONMENT
Fuel cost inflation, material prices and subcontractor rates not included. Regional living wage differences not factored into hourly rate modelling. Weather disruption, site access issues, and planning delays not captured. A complete model would integrate external cost indices for accurate forecasting.

Future Improvements & Phase 2 Roadmap

Evolving From Descriptive to Predictive Analytics

The current analysis establishes a strong descriptive foundation. Phase 2 would transition toward forecasting, profitability modelling, and automated reporting.

Profitability Modelling

HIGH IMPACT
Integrate ECO funding value per installation against operational cost to calculate programme margin by region and installation type. Transition from cost monitoring to profit optimisation — identifying which programmes are commercially viable and which require renegotiation.

Overtime Prediction Model

COST REDUCTION
Build a regression model using historical month, region, and installation volume data to predict overtime demand 4–6 weeks ahead. Enable proactive contractor pre-booking to eliminate reactive overtime spend during winter peaks.

Contractor Performance Scorecard

VENDOR MANAGEMENT
Build weighted composite scores for individual contractors combining cost per install, quality inspection outcomes, and overtime usage. Rank monthly and use as the basis for contract renewals, performance conversations, and reallocation of installation volume to top performers.

Power BI Star Schema

AUTOMATION
Migrate from flat file imports to a fully modelled star schema in Power BI with a date dimension table, employee dimension, and fact table for operational costs. Implement incremental refresh to enable automated weekly reporting without manual data exports.

Regional Benchmarking Tool

STRATEGIC PLANNING
Create a dynamic benchmarking dashboard allowing operations managers to compare any two regions across all KPIs simultaneously. Identify specific drivers of performance gaps and track improvement over time against clearly defined targets.
